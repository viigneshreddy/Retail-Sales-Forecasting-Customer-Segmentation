Methods & Concepts
Key techniques powering this dashboard — explained simply

📈 Time Series Forecasting

Retail sales data is a time series — observations ordered in time. We decompose it into trend (long-run direction), seasonality (repeating patterns like holiday spikes), and residuals (noise). Exponential smoothing methods like Holt-Winters give more weight to recent data, adapting quickly to shifts in consumer behavior.

🎯 RFM Segmentation

RFM scores each customer on three axes: Recency (how recently they bought), Frequency (how often they buy), and Monetary (how much they spend). This simple but powerful framework sorts customers into meaningful groups without needing complex ML — enabling targeted marketing that boosts ROI dramatically.

🔵 K-Means Clustering

K-Means partitions customers into k groups by minimizing within-cluster variance. We choose k by inspecting the "elbow" in the inertia curve. Once clusters are formed, each is profiled by its centroid — the average customer in that group — guiding messaging, offers, and channel strategy.

📊 MAPE & Forecast Error

Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) measures forecast accuracy as a percentage of actual values — making it interpretable across different revenue scales. A MAPE under 10% is considered excellent for retail forecasting. RMSE penalizes large errors more heavily and is useful when outliers matter.

📅 Seasonality Decomposition

Additive decomposition splits a series into Trend + Seasonality + Residual. Multiplicative decomposition uses Trend × Seasonality × Residual — better when seasonal swings grow with the trend. Identifying seasonality correctly is critical: a model blind to Q4 peaks will systematically under-forecast the most important weeks.

💡 Actionable Segmentation

Segmentation only creates value when tied to action. Champions respond to referral programs and early access. Loyal customers reward re-engagement campaigns. At-Risk customers need win-back offers with urgency. Lapsed customers often require a different channel — email open rates drop; SMS or paid retargeting can reactivate them.
